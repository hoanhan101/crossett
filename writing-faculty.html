For Faculty

Introduction:

Writing makes everyone anxious, no matter how celebrated or experienced the writer is, and teaching writing can also present many challenges. While much of teaching writing involves sitting down and explaining the difference between the dash and the hyphen, much of it is also being responsive and imaginative in order to inspire students to find their way out of fear and into their strengths as writers.

Collaboration:

Camille Guthrie, the Director of Undergraduate Writing Initiatives, is available to work with you during each term, as well as over summer and FWT breaks, to support any aspect of teaching writing in your classes. She can collaborate with you to develop practices and assignments that help students become more critical, effective, and confident writers. Here are some of the ways she can collaborate with you to support writing in your courses:

  1. Course Consultations:
    2.
    • Plan a new course, or update a previous one, with more writing activities
    • Create a new writing assignment (long or short)
    • Include a grammar or style handout in your assignments
    • Discuss new ways to integrate writing into your courses
    • Explore practices to support multi-lingual students and ELL skills
    • Learn ideas about how to respond to student writing more effectively
    • Discuss how to support students with learning differences or accommodations in your course
  2. Guest Lectures:
    3.
    • Camille can visit your class to give a brief writing or grammar workshop on a specific writing issue.
  3. Open Office Hours:
    4.
    • Camille holds open office hours three times a week to meet with students to work on their writing. Please send your students or advisees to sign up on her door (located on the third floor of Crossett Library), or email her for an appointment during these times: Tuesdays 1-4pm; Wednesday 3-5pm; Fridays 1-3pm.
    • Contact: Camille Guthrie, Director of Undergraduate Writing Initatives (Crossett Library, third floor): cguthrie@bennington.edu; ext. 4892.
