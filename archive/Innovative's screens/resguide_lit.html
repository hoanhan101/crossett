Background and Reference Sources

  • Oxford English Dictionary Online -- The Oxford English Dictionary is the accepted authority on the evolution of the English language over the last millennium. It traces the usage of words through 2.5 million quotations from a wide range of international English language sources. **Not accessible off campus**
  • The Oxford companion to classical literature. DE5.H3 1984
  • MLA handbook for writers of research papers. LB2369.G53 2003
  • An etymological dictionary of the English language. PE1580.S5 2005
  • Masterpieces of world literature, edited by Frank N. Magill. PN44.M3448 1989
  • The Norton anthology of theory and criticism. PN86.N67 2001
  • World literature criticism : 1500 to the present : a selection of major authors. PN445.W67 1992
  • Twentieth-century literary movements dictionary : a compendium to more than 500 literary, critical, and theatrical movements, schools, and groups.  PN597.T94 2000
  • The New Princeton encyclopedia of poetry and poetics. PN1021.N39 1993
  • The Cambridge guide to literature in English. PR85.C29 1993
  • The Cambridge history of American literature. PS92.C34 1994
  • Voices of multicultural America: notable speeches delivered by African, Asian, Hispanic, and Native Americans, 1790-1995. PS663.M55 V64 1996

Finding Books

  • Crossett Library Catalog -- Search our online catalog for books at Crossett Library as well as e-books accessible online.
  • WorldCat -- Millions of bibliographic records for books and other materials in libraries worldwide. Books not held by Bennington College can be requested for borrowing through Crossett Library{u2019}s Interlibrary Loan services.
  • E-server's Fiction Collection -- 50 Authors from Alcott to Melville to Woolf, full-text.
  • On-Line Book Page -- 12,000 Free, public domain full text books, most in HTML format.
  • Project Gutenberg -- The most ambitious program on the net to get all the important world literature online.

Finding Articles

  • MLA (through FirstSearch) -- The Modern Language Association International Bibliography. Provides over one million citations for items from journals and series published worldwide.
  • Academic Search Premier -- The world{u2019}s largest academic multi-disciplinary database, Academic Search Premier provides full text for more than 4,700 publications, including full text for more than 3,600 peer-reviewed journals.
  • Infotrac Expanded Academic ASAP -- Another multi-disciplinary database with article citations and selected full-text of scholarly journals, news magazines, and newspapers.
  • JSTOR -- Full-text of scholarly journals in selected areas of study, including language and literature. Fewer journals are represented here than in other databases, but many in JSTOR have a greater chronological coverage (e.g., Shakespeare Quarterly back to 1950).

Web Resources

Literary Web Indexes

  • Literary Resources on the Net -- This is a very intelligently organized, detailed list of links to original texts and related literary material.
  • Voice of the Shuttle -- An excellent guide to subject areas in the humanities.
  • PAL -- Perspectives in American Literature: A Research and Reference Guide by Paul Rueben of California State University, Stanislaus. This work is valuable as a quick and convenient reference to the major movements and various authors in American Literature. New authors and research and bibliographical information continue to be added regularly.

Online Poetry

  • American Verse Project -- An electronic archive of volumes of American poetry prior to 1920.
  • Academy of American Poets -- Founded in 1934 to support American poets at all stages of their careers and to foster the appreciation of contemporary poetry.
  • E-Server Poetry Collection -- Full-text poetry from Maya Angelou to Walt Whitman
  • Internet Poetry Archive -- Selected poems from a number of contemporary poets including Philip Levine, Seamus Heaney and Czeslaw Milosz.
  • Modern American Poetry -- Biographical, critical, analytical informaion as well as poems.
  • Representative Poetry Online -- A historical anthology, more than 1400 poems, from more than 200 authors, searchable.
