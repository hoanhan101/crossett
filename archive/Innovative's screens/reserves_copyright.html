How does copyright relate to reserves?

Course reserves are managed according to copyright law. Section 107 of the Copyright Law contains a list of reasons why the reproduction of a work might be considered {u201C}fair.{u201D} For academic purposes, these purposes may include criticism, comment, news reporting, teaching, scholarship, and research. Section 107 outlines four factors to be considered in determining whether or not a particular use is fair:

  1. the purpose and character of your use
  2. the nature of the copyrighted work
  3. the amount and substantiality of the portion taken, and
  4. the effect of the use upon the potential market.

Materials may be placed on reserve if Crossett Library deems the work a {u201C}fair use.{u201D} Please visit Crossett Library's Copyright on Campus LibGuide for more information.
