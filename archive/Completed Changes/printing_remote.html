Printing From A Laptop

PC Instructions

  1. In the Start Menu, click on the Control Panel.
  2. This will open a window. Look through the options and click Printers and Faxes.
  3. This will open another window. Click the Add a Printer option.
  4. A set up wizard will start. Click on button for Local Printer. If the option to activate a Plug and Play set up is shown, DESELECT it. Then click Next.
  5. Click on the button to create a new port, accompanied by a drop down menu for you to select the kind of new port. Select HP Standard TCP/IP Port. Then click Next.
  6. A box will pop up asking for the address of the printer. Enter the following name for the printer of your choice and click Next :
    • Enter this for LETHEM Crossett-Kyocera3500i_1
    • Enter this for TARTT Crossett-Kyocera3500i_2
  7. Select NO- you do not want this to be your default printer. Then click Next.
  8. Sometimes the printer wizard will ask if you want to share this printer. Select NO. Then click Next.
  9. Finally, you will be asked if you want to print a test page. This is up to you- yes or no. Then click Next.
  10. Click Finish.

MAC Instructions:

  1. Click on the blue apple at the top-left of the screen.
  2. Select System Preferences from the drop down menu.
  3. A window will open with several rows of options. Click on the Print and Fax icon, listed under the Hardware heading.
  4. The window will shift to a layout dominated by two large columns; the left one contains names of printers. Underneath this column are two buttons. One is labeled with a plus sign; click there.
  5. A new window will open. At the top are two options: Default Browser and IP Printer. Select IP Printer.
  6. In the line labeled address, enter the following numbers and periods for the printer of your choice:
    • Enter this for LETHEM Crossett-Kyocera3500i_1
    • Enter this for TARTT Crossett-Kyocera3500i_2
  7. Once the add button at the bottom of the window is no longer greyed out, click on it. This should close the window.
