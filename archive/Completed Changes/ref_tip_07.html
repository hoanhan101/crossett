Reference Tip:

Keyword vs. Subject Searching

An effective way to conduct research in catalogs and databases is to take advantage of both keyword and subject searching. Both types can yield relevant results depending on the topic being researched. Here is a quick run down of the strengths of the two searching styles:

Keyword SearchingSubject Searching
Keywords (Natural language words)
- allow flexible combinations
-describe your topic in your terms		Controlled Vocabulary (Subject/Topic headings)
- pre-defined headings chosen by information professionals to describe items with similar content
Catalogs and databases search for keywords anywhere in the document. The more a keyword appears in a document, the higher its displayed degree of relevancy- even if it really has nothing to do with the topic.Catalogs and databases only look for subject terms in the subject field for a document- where the most descriptive and relevant words are listed to describe an article/item.
Results can very in quantity, and relevancy can be questionable.If there are too many results, one can hone their search with sub headings. Results are always relevant to subject terms.
