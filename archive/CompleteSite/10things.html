10 Things to Know about the Library

You can have books, movies, scores, and articles from libraries all over the world delivered to you.

WorldCat is a database that contains millions of entries describing books and other materials held in thousands of libraries around the world. Using WorldCat is like browsing thousands of library catalogs at one time. If you see a title in WorldCat that Crossett does not have, you can request it through ILLiad, our system for inter-library borrowing and lending.

You can check out books over FWT and the summer.

Before you leave for Field Work Term or the summer check out some books you've been meaning to read! They won't be due until the first week you are back on campus so you'll have plenty of time to enjoy them. If you can't decide what to read check out the Faculty Suggested Reading List.

You can view the 50 Years of Janus Films in the library.

The library has the collection celebrating "the fiftieth anniversary of this world-renowned distribution company - Essential Art House: 50 Years of Janus Films, an expansive collectors' box set featuring fifty classic films on DVD and a lavishly illustrated hardcover book." It is on permanent reserve in the library and is available at the circulation desk.

You can look at amazing photography in the library.

Crossett Library has a wonderful collection of photography portfolios of original works by Edward Weston, Ansel Adams, Garry Winogrand, Eva Rubenstein, and more. Explore the complete photography collection list of works and make an appointment with Joe Tucker (x4737) to view these treasures.

You can reserve space in the library.

Students can reserve the library meeting room for informal gatherings, study groups, or meetings. The library meeting room is beautiful space on the top level of the library. It is equipped with digital projection for laptops, DVDs (both domestic and foreign format) and VHS. If you would like to schedule the library meeting room please contact Laura x4610.

You can exhibit your art and projects in the library.

Students can display art or projects in the library. There are several exhibition space possibilities. If you would like to exhibit your work in the library please contact Laura Niles x4610.

You can check out free admission passes to the Clark Art Museum and MassMoCA.

The library has passes for two adult admissions to the Clark Art Museum and MassMoCA. The passes can be checked out for one week and are available at the circulation desk.

You can find your great grandmother's cousin's daughter.

Ancestry Library Database of census, immigration, birth, death, marriage, court, land, and military records. Contains city directories, newspapers and access to over 4 billion names.

You can get classical music scores online.

Classical Scores Library contains pages of classical scores from both in-copyright and public domain editions. The major composers' output is represented, as well as many lesser-known composers and works.

You can indulge in some noetical fun.

That's right, noetical, which means "purely intellectual". You can learn about the origins of this and countless other great words and expressions in the online version of the Oxford English Dictionary.
