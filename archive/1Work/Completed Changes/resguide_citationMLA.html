E-Books | Articles / Exerpts | Web pages

Books

Follow this pattern when citing books:

Author's name. Title of book. Place of publication: Publisher name, date. Format.
(Please note that the format for hard-copy backs is "print".)

Book -- one author

Hall, Donald. The happy man: poems. New York: Random House, 1986. Print.

Book -- two or more authors

Barenboim, Daniel, Edward W. Said, and Ara Guzelimian. Parallels and Paradoxes: Explorations in Music and Society. New York:
Scribner, 2004. Print.

Multi-volume work -- more than three authors / editors

Davis, Alva L. et al., eds. A compilation of the work sheets of the linguistic atlas of the United States and Canada and associated
projects. 1 vol. Chicago: University of Chicago Press, 1969. Print.

Edited book

Poe, Edgar Allen. Selected tales. Ed. Julian Symons. Oxford: Oxford University Press, 1980. Print.

E-Books

Follow the same pattern as when citing books in print, substituting "Internet resource" for "Print" format.

Reuter, Thomas Anton. Custodians of the sacred mountains: culture and society in the highlands of Bali. Honolulu: University of
Hawai'i Press, 2002. Internet Resource.

Articles / Excerpts

Follow this general pattern for articles:

Author's name. "Title of article." Name of journal, volume number, (year of publication): page numbers of article.
Medium of publication. (For online retrieval, also note date of access).

Journal article

Shackelford, Dean. "The Female Voice in 'To Kill a Mockingbird': Narrative Strategies in Film and Novel." The Mississippi Quarterly
50 (1996): 101-13. Print.

Online journal article

Fishman, Ethan. "Not Compassionate, Not Conservative." American Scholar 76 (2007): 48-55. Web. 22 January 2007 .

Poem

Kasdorf, Julia. "Mennonites." Sleeping preacher. Pittsburgh: University of Pittsburgh Press, 1992. 34-34. Print.

Chapter in a book

McCorkle, Jill. "The Andy Griffith Show." Prime times: writers on their favorite TV shows. New York: Three Rivers Press, 2004. 60-
66. Print.

Entry in a reference work

"Privacy." New Dictionary of the History of Ideas. New York: Charles Scribner's Sons, 2005. Print.
Note: for better-known reference works, include edition information, but omit full publication information. For less familiar works, include full publication information. When in doubt, include full publication information.

Web pages

For citing web pages, MLA does not require the listing of URLs. While web pages vary greatly and authoritative information may not be available, you should try to include in your citation.

Name of author or editor. "Article title". Name of the website, Date, issue or version number. Name of publisher. Medium (which
is Web). Date of access.

Groeger, Lena. "What the frack is in that water?" Propublica, 7 March 2012. Web. 11 March 2012.
