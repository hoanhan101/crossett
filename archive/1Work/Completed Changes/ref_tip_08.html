Reference Tip:

Domains

One way to identify websites which would be useful for your research is by domain type. Domains suffixes, also referred to as top level domains indicate what type of group or ogranization is responsible for the website in question. The type of domain can indicate the intention or purpose behind the sites presentation of content.

For example, a commercial website is most likely going to want to present itself and its products in the best manner possible in order to make sure that it stays in business. Organization Sites can be non biased, but can just as likely be a private, extremely partisan orgaization whose intent is to its own particulat view point. Government sites provide offical government opinions and resrources.

Use the domain types as a flag to indicate to you from what angle the group/ organization may potentially be taking in presenting your content, and keep that in mind as you review its materials for your research purposes.

.com = Commercial Site
.edu = Educational Site
.gov = Government Site
.net = Network Service Providers
.org = Organization Site. Including,
  but not exclusively, non-profits.
.us, .ie,
.uk		 = Country Codes
