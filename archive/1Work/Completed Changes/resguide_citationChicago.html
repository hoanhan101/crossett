Chicago "Humanities" Style Sample Bibliography Entries

E-Books | Journal Articles | Web Sites

Here is a printer-friendly PDF version of these examples.

Books

Follow this pattern when citing books:

Author's name.Title of book. Place of publication: Publisher name, Date.

Book -- one author

Briggs, Robin. Early modern France 1560-1715. Oxford: Oxford University Press, 1977.

Two authors

Cole, G.D.H., and Raymond Postgate. The British people, 1746-1946. London: Barnes & Noble, 1961.

Editor, translator, or compiler instead of author

Shreve, Susan Richards and Porter Shreve, eds. Outside the law: narratives on justice in America. Boston: Beacon Press, 1997.

Editor, translator, or compiler in addition to author

Gardner, John. On writers and writing. Edited by Stewart O'Nan. Cambridge, Mass.: Perseus, 1994.

Chapter or other part of a book

Bohlman, Philip V. "The remembrance of things past: music, race and the end of history in modern Europe." In Music and the Racial Imagination, edited by Ronald Radano and Philip V. Bohlman, 644-669. Chicago: University of Chicago Press, 2000.

E-Books

Follow the same pattern as when citing books in print, with the addition of online retrieval information as shown below.

Bayers, Peter L. Imperial ascent: mountaineering, masculinity, and empire. Boulder, Colo.: University Press of Colorado, 2003. http://site.ebrary.com/lib/avic/Doc?id=10069581

Journal Articles

Follow this pattern for journal articles:

Author's name. Title of article. Name of journal, volume number, issue number, (year of publication): page numbers of article.

Article in a print journal

Suppe, Frederick. "The structure of a scientific paper." Philosophy of Science 65, no. 3 (1998): 381-405.

Online journal from a database (such as EBSCOhost)

Woods, Kerry D. "Effects of Invasion by Lonicera tatarica L. On Herbs and Tree Seedlings in Four New England Forests." American Midland Naturalist 130, no. 1 (1993): 62-74. In JSTOR [database online]. Cited 05 February 2007. Available from Bennington College Library.

Web Sites

For Web sites, follow this order as closely as possible:

Name of author or parent organization. "Title of document." Title of complete work (if relevant). Date of publication or last revision. [URL]. (Date of access).

United States Department of State. "Background note: Iran." Country Background Notes. Updated October 2006. [http://www.state.gov/r/pa/ei/bgn/5314.htm]. (January 19, 2007).
