Avoiding Plagiarism

A citation is a form of reference which identifies or gives credit to a book, article, web page or any other information source used by the writer of a research paper. Citations may take the form of footnotes throughout a paper, and/or in a list of sources called a bibliography. By providing citations in the form of footnotes or bibliography, you acknowledge your sources of information and allow the reader the option of finding additional material on your topic.

Common Citation Styles

Some of the most frequently used citation formats are APA, Chicago, and MLA styles.

NOTE: Click on a citation style to see bibliographic examples.

APA style (based on the Publication Manual of the American Psychological Association) is the preferred format for papers in psychology, education, and the social sciences.

Chicago style (based on The Chicago manual of style) is a comprehensive guide to citation but also to matters of grammar and writing style. Chicago style is often used for history papers, but is a good general guide that can be used in any academic discipline.

MLA style (based on the Modern Language Association handbook for writers of research papers) is the preferred choice for research papers in literature, the arts, and humanities.

If you are not sure which citation style to follow, ask your teacher for advice. Whatever style you decide to use, be sure to use that format for the entire paper.
