Select Databases

  • PsycARTICLES -- Search PsycARTICLES for full text of articles from APA journals and EPF (Educational Publishing Foundation) journals, most from 1985 to the present.
  • PsycINFO -- PsycINFO contains nearly 2.3 million citations and summaries of scholarly journal articles, book chapters, books, and dissertations, all in psychology and related disciplines, dating as far back as the 1800s.
  • PubMed -- This resource covers articles published in 3900 biomedical journals.
  • JSTOR -- A database of scholarly journal articles including illustrations in literature, social sciences, mathematics, arts, and sciences. A much greater range of dates is represented than in most databases.
  • EBSCO Academic Search Premier -- Multidisciplinary database containing full text articles for nearly 4,700 publications, as well as indexing and abstracts for 8,172 journals.
  • INFOTRAC Expanded Academic ASAP -- Database covering social sciences, the humanities, science and technology, national news, and general interest magazine.
  • Proquest -- Search one or more subject specific databases that Proquest provides. Subjects such as Biology, Computing, Education, Health Management, Psychology, Social Science, and major U.S. Newspapers are available through this resource.

Search Strategies

For databases

  1. Conduct an advanced search.
    1. Have {u201C}Social Psychology{u201D} as your subject.
    2. Relate the subject and your keywords with a Boolean operator: and, or, not
    3. Enter in any other potential subject headings I.E. {u201C}Healthcare{u201D}, {u201C}African Americans{u201D}, {u201C}Israel{u201D}, {u201C}Pakistan{u201D}, etc
    4. Relate these with keywords that specifically describe your topic.
  2. If your results are too narrow, start eradicating lines from your search. First eradicate the keywords, then some of the subject headings.
  3. Try to rearrange your search to see if your relevancy vs. recall is affected.

Search within a journal

  • Locate a specific journal that deals with Social Psychology. Some databases will allow you to conduct searches within the holdings of a specific journal. Search within titles with your keywords that describe your topic.

Check the bibliographies

  1. If you have a particular article that you find specifically relevant to your topic, look to see if you can locate the articles in the bibliography. Most likely, these articles/items will also be relevant to your topic.
