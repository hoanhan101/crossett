When you conduct a search for books in the library catalog, you may find that your search results contain some titles that are listed as EBOOKS. These selections will have the words [electronic resource] after the book's main title. These e-books are electronic versions of the original print version of the book. By adding e-books to its collection, Crossett Library can offer its readers access to many more titles than we could possibly house in our building. Additional benefits of e-books are 24/7 online access and the ability to search the entire content of a book.

You can locate e-books in the catalog by looking for the EBOOKS designation on the right side of the results screen. If you want to find only e-books, you can do an advanced search and limit your material type to e-books.

Ebrary and NetLibrary

Crossett's e-book holdings derive from two databases- Ebrary and NetLibrary. Ebrary contains over 24,000 full-text academic books, while NetLibrary offers digital full-text versions of more than 1,000 reference works, scholarly monographs, literature and fiction, as well as 3,400 publicly-accessible titles.

Ebrary

In order to access Ebrary titles, you will need to download the free Ebrary reader. This software only takes a few seconds to download, and you do not need to re-start your computer, although you may need to re-start your web browser. For the Ebrary reader to display properly, make sure that cookies are enabled and the pop-up blocker is turned off on your web browser. Please be patient when you open a book; sometimes it takes a long time for the Ebrary Reader to load.

Troubleshooting: Having problems with loading the ebrary reader? Try these steps:

  1. Click on the Ebrary link.
  2. Click the HELP link. It is located in the top-left corner, under the word Bennington.
  3. A pop up window will appear that will have a variety of help options. Click Installing the Ebrary Reader.
  4. Read the section that is shown regarding Installation. Click on either PC Installer or MAC Installer, depending on the type of computer yopu have.
  5. Follow the steps provided in the Installation Wizard.
  6. NOTE:
    7. When you reopen Ebrary, Nothing will necessarily look different. When you try to open a book, you will be able to know whether the reader installed or not.

If you are still having problems, please contact Jared Della Rocca or Joe Tucker .

Net Library

NetLibrary works with most popular browsers. Adobe Reader 4.0 or higher is required to view eBooks online, while Adobe Reader 6.0 or higher is required to download and view full-text eContent offline.

If you do not have an Adobe Reader, you can download a copy for free.

If you are having problems, please contact Jared Della Rocca or Joe Tucker .

Troubleshooting Ebrary

Ebrary Login/Bookshelf Problems

Problem: You click on either the login link or the bookshelf tab and get an error message mentioning a problem with the security certificate/problem verifying that the security certificate belongs to the website.

Solution: Depending on the browser you are using, you will have an option listed- as either as a button or a link- that allows you to accept the certificate and continue onto the site ("OK", "Continue", "Add this site to my list of exceptions", etc.). Click to accept the security certificate. You should never see this error message from the browser you are using after this.

If you are having problems, please contact Jared Della Rocca or Joe Tucker .
