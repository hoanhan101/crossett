Reference Tip:

Primary VS. Secondary Sources

A primary source is first- hand information about an event derived from an individual who either participated or witnessed it.

Examples- Offical Documents and records
Period newspapers
Statistics
Diaries, Correspondances, and Memoirs
Interviews, Oral histories, Personal Narratives
Scientific Reports directly from group
     who conducted the research

A secondary source is information derived from a person who relied on primary sources to compile and create their information on an event. They tend to be works which reorganize, reinterpret, summarise, index or in some fashion "add value" to the information reported in the primary sources.

Examples- Current newspaper articles about a past event.
Biographies
Bibliographies
Textbooks
Handbooks and Compilations
